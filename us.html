Experiment

For this project, we wanted to work with wearable-tech to build a tactile, experimental and performative installation piece. Using soft sensors and found materials, we created a garment that was an intervention model in a speculative future. In this future, following a bio-arm war, humans no longer touched one another. The garments were built (by an outsider) to encourage curious interactions and human connection amongst these humans. However, it was important for us to state that this was a prescriptive solution where these humans were being forced to interact with one another in the “normal” way. This piece challenges how important touch is to the human experience and considers different ways of reimagining connection.

Sheer Contact was created within seven weeks by three creative technologists: Anna Pragman, Krithi Nalla, Tanvi Mishra
